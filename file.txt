In 2021, Apple Inc. announced a strategic partnership with IBM and Amazon to accelerate innovation in cloud computing. 
Tim Cook, the CEO of Apple, said during the conference in San Francisco that the company plans to invest over $5 billion 
in developing new data centers in the United States and Europe. 

Meanwhile, Microsoft launched its new Surface Pro 9 and Windows 12 preview at a global event in London, attended by 
over 10,000 developers from 35 countries. Satya Nadella emphasized the importance of artificial intelligence and 
sustainable technology, mentioning that by 2030, Microsoft aims to become carbon negative.

In the Middle East, the Dubai Future Foundation announced the opening of a new AI Research Center in collaboration 
with Stanford University. The center will focus on machine learning applications in healthcare, education, and transportation. 

Elon Musk also made headlines after SpaceX successfully launched the Falcon 9 rocket carrying 60 Starlink satellites. 
NASA officials congratulated the team, noting that this marks the 85th successful mission in the past two years. 

According to a recent report by Reuters, global tech investment reached $300 billion in 2024, a 12% increase compared 
to the previous year. Analysts predict that 2025 will see even greater growth driven by advances in quantum computing 
and natural language processing.

Finally, Google DeepMind revealed its new AI model, AlphaSolve, which can predict molecular interactions with unprecedented accuracy. 
The company claims the model could reduce drug discovery costs by 40%, significantly impacting the pharmaceutical industry.
